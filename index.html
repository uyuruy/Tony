Case Study: LifeFleet → OneBox → OneSystem

LifeBank • Government primary healthcare supply chain • Live across Kaduna, Yobe & Kano

LifeFleet OneBox OneSystem (OneFleet + OneBox + OneWallet)

The Strategy

I designed a deliberate low-friction entry, then expanded into full-stack infrastructure:

  • LifeFleet (enter low): A fleet-management system for government health agencies (DMAs), moving products from central stores to facilities. Prove we manage their assets better — lower cost, higher reliability — with a small team.
  • OneBox (expand): Last-mile distribution to facilities with visibility, AI demand prediction, and local 3PL delivery. This is where donor funding came in.
  • OneWallet (complete): The financing layer — requisition funding and payments — closing the loop into an end-to-end system.

My Role

I architected and deployed the initial phase of this backward, end-to-end integration. On consolidation into OneSystem, LifeFleet was rebranded OneFleet, joined by OneBox and OneWallet into one interoperable stack for primary healthcare centres.

Case Study: SmartCentre → SmartBank

LifeBank • Maternal health & blood safety • Deployed across Nigeria, Kenya & Sierra Leone; full tiered SmartBank system in Sierra Leone

The Challenge

Improve maternal health outcomes by providing reliable, local access to safe blood in regions with limited advanced health infrastructure — and make the facility sustainable beyond launch.

What I Built — A Tiered System

I redesigned a standalone screening lab into a three-layer system, and renamed the product SmartBank:

  • SmartCentre: the physical container — the lab itself.
  • The Technology: the platform that runs screening, processes, and data.
  • The Operations Layer: the system that brings blood in and out, plus the people, project team, and processes that keep the centre running sustainably long-term. None of this existed before my redesign.

Case Study: AI-Powered Market Risk Analysis

Project Overview

An autonomous, serverless workflow I designed and built from scratch to automate market risk analysis. It fetches daily news headlines, uses Google's Gemini AI to produce a structured risk assessment, and distributes the intelligence to a live Airtable dashboard and a proactive Asana task system.

My Role & Architecture

As sole architect, I coded the end-to-end pipeline in Google Apps Script. The workflow:

  1. Triggers daily to fetch news from pre-configured RSS feeds.
  2. Sends headlines to the Gemini API within an engineered prompt returning strict JSON.
  3. Distributes structured data via API to an Airtable dashboard and an Asana project for proactive alerts.

Live System View

The live Airtable database below is the central hub for the daily, AI-generated risk analysis.

Key Challenges & Resolutions

  • API Errors (404s & 403s): Resolved model rejection and access errors by iteratively testing model versions and re-configuring token permission scopes.
  • Data Parsing Failures: Implemented robust regex extraction to guarantee clean JSON, overcoming AI response formatting inconsistencies.

Internsify Africa — Product Management Program

Curriculum Designer & Lead Instructor • Paid cohort, contract • In-person + remote, Lagos • ~13 weeks (Apr–Jul 2026)

The Brief

Take a small group of motivated, early-career students with no formal PM background and make them job-ready in real product thinking over a single intensive program. I owned the full arc: curriculum, every teaching artifact, live sessions, the feedback loop, and the capstone.

My Ownership

  • Curriculum design: built the full multi-week arc from scratch.
  • Proprietary framework: developed the teaching system the course runs on.
  • All materials: session boards, task cards, instructor notes, student guides, capstone briefs.
  • Live delivery & 1:1 coaching: led every session, calibrated feedback to each student.
  • Stakeholder management: owned scope, timeline, and outcomes with program leadership.

The Framework I Built

A repeatable way to reason about product decisions, reinforced through live application:

  • Read — four signals off real pilot data: Outcome, Output, Counter, Qualitative.
  • Decide — Scale, Iterate, Kill, or Extend, pressure-tested through Clarity, Evidence, Completeness.
  • Communicate — a five-block decision memo (BLUF first), tradeoffs along four axes.
  • Defend — defending the call live under challenge: the muscle that separates theory from doing the job.

Outcomes

  • A complete PM curriculum delivered from a blank page, on a consistent design system.
  • Students moved from zero PM background to running the full decision loop and defending it live.
  • Each student drove an end-to-end capstone — real user interviews through a written spec — producing demonstrable portfolio work.
  • Strongest student reached interview-ready standard.

Psalms Laundry — A Business Built to Run Without Me

Founder & Director • Subscription laundry & cleaning, Yaba, Lagos • CAC incorporated 2026

The Problem & Approach

A real Lagos gap: reliable, quality laundry at predictable prices with zero customer-side logistics. I ran it informally for ~2 years, then rebuilt it as a system-driven, subscription-first business with documented SOPs, a trained team, and a live operations app — designed so it runs without me.

Systems I Built

  • Live mobile ops app (AppSheet): three field forms — pickup, sorting, QC — with four live automations driving order status.
  • Financial model: a 31-tab workbook with monthly P&L and a weighted piece-count system built from unit economics up.
  • SOP library: intake, washing, QC, packaging, delivery, and complaints, plus a pricing handbook and 37-type unit-counting guide.
  • Customer & order database: 5 linked tables, 59 customers, order-level breakdowns.
